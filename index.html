Why multiply by 12?

The drag formula assumes one campaign cycle per month — 12 launch cycles per year. Each absorbs the same delay. The number of dev-dependent campaigns per month drives the campaigns unlocked and marketer capacity metrics, not this drag figure.

Why is the drag larger than the net return?

The drag figure is the theoretical maximum — what you lose assuming 100% of the delay converts to lost revenue. The net return applies two conservative discounts: 40% adoption (not every workflow moves to Uniform immediately) and 75% revenue attribution (not every speed gain converts directly to revenue). The drag is the ceiling; the net return is the conservative floor.
